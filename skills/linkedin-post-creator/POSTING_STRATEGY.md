# 📝 POSTING_STRATEGY.md

## 🚀 The 2x/Day System

**Post 2 times per day** — morning and afternoon. This maximizes visibility and reach.

### The Daily Rhythm
| Time | Content Type |
|------|--------------|
| **Morning** | How-To, Insight, or Proof (Educational) |
| **Afternoon** | Opinion, Myth-Buster, or Swipe File (Engagement) |

### Weekly Breakdown (10 posts/week)
- Monday: How-To + Opinion
- Tuesday: Proof + Myth-Buster
- Wednesday: How-To + Swipe File
- Thursday: Insight + Vulnerability
- Friday: Proof + Opinion

---

## 🎯 4 Core Content Types

### 1. How-To Posts
Teach something useful they can act on immediately.

- Think: "How we 2x'd reply rates without changing our offer"
- Keep it specific, short, and actionable
- No "thought leadership energy"

**Why it works:** Gets shared, saved, and "stolen" (which is a compliment).

### 2. Insight Posts
Share a sharp take, spicy opinion, or something that makes your ICP stop scrolling.

- What trend is overrated?
- What mistake do you see people make every week?
- What do most people misunderstand about your space?

**Why it works:** This is your "I've been in the trenches" energy.

### 3. Proof Posts
Show that you know your stuff, without saying "I'm an expert."

- Screenshot of a real result
- Client win, small or big
- "This worked for me" story, told like a casual chat

**Why it works:** No humblebrag needed, just show what's possible.

### 4. Vulnerability Posts
This isn't about oversharing. It's about being a real person.

- "Here's a massive life change I made last week"
- "This flopped, and what I learned"
- "This transition was hard, here's how I got through it"

**Why it works:** Honest posts = magnetic posts. They build trust faster than case studies.

## 📝 5 Proven Frameworks (Plug-and-Play)

### Framework 1: "This Worked for Me" Post
**Template:**
```
Last month, we {specific action}.

Here's what happened: {result}.

Here's what we learned.

If you're {ICP}, you might want to try this.
```

**Example:**
```
Last month, we tested a 3-line DM script with no pitch.

Reply rates jumped from 12% to 38%.

Turns out, asking someone if they're open to chat > telling them why you're great.

If you're running LinkedIn outreach, don't overcomplicate the opener.
```

**Why it works:** It's humble. It's useful. It feels like something you'd text a friend.

---

### Framework 2: The Myth-Buster
Challenge conventional wisdom. Call out outdated advice. Be the person who says what everyone's thinking.

**Template:**
```
Everyone says {thing everyone says}.

Here's why that's wrong.

Here's what actually works (and proof).
```

**Example:**
```
Everyone says "personalize your cold DMs."

But most personalization looks like this: "Saw you went to {college}!"

That's not personalization. That's LinkedIn Mad Libs.

Instead, comment on something they care about, like their recent post or hiring status.
```

**Why it works:** Hooks attention and positions you as someone with experience (not just opinions).

---

### Framework 3: "Here's What No One Talks About"
People love behind-the-scenes content.

**Template:**
```
Everyone shares the wins. Let's talk about the part no one posts about:

{Insert the messy middle, the failure, or the awkward lesson}.

Here's what I learned and what I'd do differently next time.
```

**Example:**
```
Everyone posts when their campaign books 30 meetings.

No one posts about the 3 that tanked.

One of ours flopped because we used the same icebreaker across industries.

It reminded me that even good copy dies with bad targeting.
```

**Why it works:** It's real. And people trust real.

---

### Framework 4: The Swipe File Post
Hand people something they can steal. You become useful and memorable.

**Template:**
```
Steal this {template, script, checklist}.

We've used it to {result}.

Feel free to make it yours.

And if you do, let me know how it goes.
```

**Example:**
```
Steal this DM opener:

"Hey {FirstName}, not sure if now's the right time, but I help {ICP} with {outcome}. Worth a quick chat sometime?"

Short, respectful, non-cringe. It's working like crazy right now.
```

**Why it works:** You're providing immediate value with zero strings attached.

---

### Framework 5: The Punchy POV Post
Share a belief. Keep it short. Let people agree, disagree, or get curious.

**Template:**
```
{Strong opinion in 1–2 sentences}.

Here's why I believe it.

What do you think?
```

**Example:**
```
If your cold DM doesn't sound like a text message, it's too long.

People don't want to read essays from strangers.

Short, sharp, relevant. That's the game.
```

**Why it works:** It invites conversation without needing a long story.

## 🔄 Repurposing Workflow (Squeeze Every Post)

Once you write a post, repurpose it:

### 1. Turn into an Email
That how-to post? Drop it into your newsletter or send as one-off email to leads. Change first line, add subject like "This bumped our reply rate by 3x."

### 2. Pull a DM Snippet
Take the best line from your post and use it as part of your outbound sequence.

Example: "Last week I shared a post about how we booked 7 meetings with one 3-line message. Happy to send it over if you're curious."

### 3. Use in Outreach Campaign
If the post performed well, take the core message and turn it into a first-touch icebreaker or follow-up.

### 4. Comment Bait
Quote a line from your post in a comment on a prospect's post. Add your perspective. Build visibility with zero extra effort.

### 5. Record as Short Video
Not feeling like a writer? Cool. Take your best-performing post, turn on camera, say it out loud. Same message, new format, different engagement type.

## 💬 Commenting Strategy (Highest ROI Activity)

If you want visibility but short on time, **commenting beats posting**.

### The 10-Minute Daily Comment Habit

**Block 10 minutes daily. Do this:**

1. **Comment on 3 posts from your ICPs** — Even 10-like posts are gold if from right person. Goal = visibility, not engagement.

2. **Add 1 thoughtful comment on an industry influencer's post** — Go where crowd is. Drop something that adds to convo (no "Great point!" stuff).

3. **Engage 1 warm prospect you're nurturing** — Got someone who ghosted? Comment on their latest post. No pitch, just show up and be human.

### How to Comment Effectively

**1. Be specific, not generic**
- Instead of: "Totally agree."
- Try: "Interesting take, especially the part about multichannel outreach. We've seen similar results when we added LinkedIn touches to cold email flows."

**2. Ask a question**
- Shows curiosity, invites dialogue, makes post creator more likely to reply and check you out.
- Example: "This makes a lot of sense. Curious, have you tried this with mid-market vs enterprise leads? Wondering how response rates differ."

**3. Add a quick insight or story**
- Example: "Completely agree on cold DMs needing to feel like a conversation. We tested a 'quick question' opener and saw a 2.5x bump in replies."

**4. Use AI to help (but not to fake it)**
- Stuck? Paste the post into ChatGPT and ask for 3 smart comments in your tone. Edit to sound like you.
